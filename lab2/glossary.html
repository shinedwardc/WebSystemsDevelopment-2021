  • Abridge - to reduce in scope; diminish
  • Adjourn - to suspend indefinitely or until a later stated time
  • Adjournment - state or interval of being adjourned
  • Apportion - to divide and share out according to plan; to make a proportionate division or distribution of
  • Ascertain - to make certain, exact, or precise; to find out or learn with certainty
  • Capitation - direct uniform tax imposed on each head or person
  • Cession - yielding to another
  • Commission - group of people who have been given the official job of finding information about something or controlling something; an amount of money paid to an employee for selling something
  • Concur - to agree with something or someone
  • Concurrence - agreement or union in action, opinion, or design; coincidence of equal powers in law
  • Construe - to understand or explain the sense or intention of, usually in a particliar way or with respect to a given set of circumstances
  • Continuance - adjournment of a course case to a further day; the extent of continuing
  • Convict - to find or prove to be guilty; to find a defendant guilty
  • Deem - to come to think or judge; to have an opinion
  • Deprive - to take something away from; to withhold something from
  • Emolument - returns arising from office or employment usually in the form of compensation or prerequisites
  • Enumeration - act or process of making or stating a list of things one after another; the act of countig something or a count made of something
  • Equity - fairness or justice in the way people are treated
  • Excises - internal tax levied on the manufacture, sale, or consumption of a commodity; any of various taxes on privileges often assessed in the form of a license or fee
  • Ex Post Facto - after the fact; done, made, or formulated after the fact
  • Delegate - (n) person acting for another: such as a representative to a convention or conference; (v) to appoint as one's representative
  • Devolve - to pass on (something, such as responsibility, rights, or powers) from one person or entity to another
  • Disparage - to depreciate by indirect means; speak slightingly about; to lower in rank or reputation
  • Forfeiture - the loss of property or money because of a breach of a legal obligation; the act of forfeiting
  • Grievance - cause of distress felt to afford reason for complaint or resistance
  • Habeas Corpus - any or several common-law writs issued to bring a party before a court or judge; the right of a citizen to obtain a writ of habeas corpus against illegal imprisonment
  • Imposts - something imposed or levied
  • Indictment - formal written statement framed by a prosecuting authority and found by a jury charging a person with an offense
  • Infringe - to encroach upon in a way that violates law or the rights of another
  • Jurisdiction - power, right, or authority to interpret and apply the law
  • Levy - the imposition or collection of an assessment; an amount of money that must be paid and that is collected by a government or other authority
  • Naturalize - to confer the rights of a nation on; to introduce into common use or into vernacular; to cause to become established as if native
  • Posterity - offspring of one progenitor to the furthest generation; all future generations
  • Prescribe - to lay down as a guide, direction, or rule of action; to specifiy with authority
  • Pro Tempore - for the time being
  • Prosecution - the party by whom criminal proceedings are instituted or conducted; institution and continuance of a criminal suit involving the process of pursuing formal charges against an offender to final judgement
  • Quarter - to provide with lodging or shelter
  • Ratification - the act or process of ratifying something (such as a treaty or amendment); formal confirmation or sanction
  • Redress - to set right; to make up for; to remove the cause of (a grievance or complaint)
  • Quorum - number (such as a majority) of officers or members of a body that when duly assembled is legally competent to transact business
