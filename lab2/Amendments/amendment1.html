Amendment 1

Original Wording:

Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof; or abridging the freedom of speech, or of the press; or the right of the people peaceably to assemble, and to petition the Government for a redress of grievances.

Summary:

The first amendment guarantees five key freedoms to the American people: religion, speech, press, assembly, and petition. Freedom of religion allows all citizens to create and follow whatever religions they want to. Freedom of speech and press allow citizens to say and print information without government oversight. Freedom of assembly and petition give citizens to ask for governmental change, both by gathering in public spaces, and formal signed petitions. These "five freedoms" have been key to many Supreme Court cases, and interpretations of this amendment continue to shape and protect American's freedoms.
